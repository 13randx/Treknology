WHAT IT'S ALL ABOUT

Science fiction has been a repository for imaginative concepts for some time now, as either a foretelling of future technology or merely an influence. “It is now received wisdom that Western science fiction anticipated or even sparked future inventions. Famous examples are the prediction of debit cards in Edward Bellamy’s 1888 novel Looking Backward, antidepressants in Aldous Huxley’s 1931 Brave New World, and the first internet search engine in the 1978 BBC radio comedy The Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy” (Jacobs, S. 2015).

Either way there has been numerous instances where a marvelous piece of equipment or handy gizmo had been introduced by TV shows and movies, only to become a real artifact for use today.

During the 60’s when Star Trek originated, most of the technology on the show was far fetched science fiction, based little on actual scientific fact, and some were just a solution to a small budget or necessity driven (O’Connor, S. 2014). Yet some of these far fetched concepts have actually been produced or are even currently in production (Brown, S. & Logan, B. 2015).

The point of this website is to explore a timeline based exploration of Star Trek technology, affectionately refered to as Treknology. The tech on the site comes from The Original Series (TOS), though improved in The Next Generation (TNG) and Voyager (VOY), but the real-world versions of this tech mostly drew inspriration from the show. It hadn’t always been ivented on TOS per say, but the popularization was often enough to wet the appettites of budding scientists, inventors and dreamers.

A trip through space is also a jaunt through the timeline of the real world tech available today. Even if TOS hadn’t necessarily invented the ideas of the future, it had a big hand in predicting and designing it. Gene Roddenbery, as with many other science fiction writers, was a visionary that could see the potential of possibility, and had an understanding of what was to come.

Looking forward, what’s next? Talk among scientists hypothesize that teleportation (or beaming) is already being explored. Warp speed may still be purely hypothetical, but augmented reality and smart operating systems with voice command are a work in progress. Phasers are still a work fo fiction, although laser weapons are alegedly already being tested by the US military. Great strides are being made in robotics, so having a Data on the bridge may not be too far away. Universal translaters are in effect in baby stages, and NASA has begun work on the IXS Enterprise in preparation for space exploration by the turn of the century.

Some of the predictions of Star Trek already in use, though not currently featured here, are Google Glass, sliding doors, flashdrives, biometrics, GPS, and personal computers. Perhaps these and other items could make an appearance in the next update.
